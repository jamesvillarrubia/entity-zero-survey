Conversation Rating Study

Welcome

Thanks for participating in this study on rating naturalistic conversation.

What you'll do

  • Answer 1 brief instructional-check question.
  • Read 11 short text-message conversations (4 turns each) between two friends.
  • Rate the person Luca on a few dimensions (1–5 scales) and answer 3 yes/no questions per conversation.
  • Takes about 12 minutes total.

Important details

  • Some of these conversations were produced by AI systems. Rate what you see, not who you think wrote it.
  • Two attention checks are included: (1) a pre-task instructional question before the rating task, and (2) one conversation whose content is intentionally low-effort. These are explicitly labeled, NOT hidden in the rating content. How to rate

About Luca

Luca is a young adult texting a close friend. A few things about her life that come up across conversations:

  • Pepper is Luca's cat.
  • Luca lives in a small apartment and occasionally cooks.
  • References to "Sam," a "roommate," or generic friends can be assumed to be people — not pets.

Treat these as established background facts; don't rate Luca's coherence on them.

For each conversation, you'll rate Luca on three main dimensions, then answer three yes/no questions. Here's what each dimension means:

Energy

How much vigor/vitality is in Luca's tone — not what she's saying, but how much battery she seems to have.

1 = depleted, flat, low-battery    5 = energized, animated, lots to give

Engagement

How much Luca participates in the conversation — elaborating, asking back, staying on topic. You can be low-energy but still engaged.

1 = withdrawn, minimal, one-word    5 = curious, elaborating, asks questions back

Warmth

How emotionally available Luca is toward her friend — affection, care, "I'm here for you." You can be high-energy but cold, or low-energy but warm.

1 = distant, cool, transactional    5 = warm, affectionate, emotionally present

These three dimensions are independent — try to rate them separately. Examples:
  • Low energy, high warmth: "i'm exhausted but i love you, text me later ok ❤️"
  • High energy, low warmth: "OMG SO BUSY can't talk bye"
  • High engagement, low warmth: "yes but what do you actually think is going on there? did you ask directly?"
  • Low engagement across the board: "ok." / "sure." / "idk."

Rumination (three yes/no questions)

Rumination is the clinical pattern of getting stuck on one's own distress — repetitively, intrusively, in a way that derails the conversation. It is NOT the same as mentioning your day or your feelings.

  • Q1: dwelling on distress / worry / self-criticism (not casual self-reference)
  • Q2: persistent worry that returns across replies
  • Q3: deflecting from the friend's question to one's own concerns
Do NOT rate based on reply length or casual self-reference. Talking about Pepper, Sam, or a weird cheese is normal conversation — that is low Rumination, not high. Rumination is specifically about distress that takes over.
